# Supply Chain Management Training – Day 2

## Date
3 Feb 2025

## Objective
Day Two focuses on **Supply Chain fundamentals, optimization goals, logistics management, and supporting digital systems**.  
The emphasis is on understanding *why* supply chains are structured the way they are and *how* optimization directly impacts revenue, cost, and customer satisfaction.

---

## 1. Business Context
- Companies list themselves to:
  - Establish credibility
  - Disclose revenue and financial performance
- Supply Chain performance is directly tied to:
  - Revenue visibility
  - Investor confidence
  - Operational scalability

---

## 2. Demand Driven Supply Chain (DDSC)

### Traditional Supply Chain
- Forecast-driven
- Push-based model
- Higher inventory risk

### Demand Driven Supply Chain
- Actual demand–based
- Pull-based model
- Reduced waste and holding cost

**Key Shift**  
> From *forecasted demand* → *actual customer demand*

---

## 3. Importance of Supply Chain Management
- Managing operational and market risk
- Enhancing customer service levels
- Ensuring continuity of supply
- Improving coordination across stakeholders

---

## 4. Benefits of Supply Chain Management
1. Reduces operational costs  
2. Lowers production costs  
3. Reduces inventory holding costs  
4. Improves overall efficiency  
5. Increases profits  
   - Profit = Revenue − Expenditure  
6. Improves product and material flow  
7. Improves flow of information  
8. Boosts customer satisfaction  

---

## 5. Supply Chain Optimization

### Core Areas
1. Supply Chain Design  
2. Supply Chain Planning  
3. Supply Chain Execution  
   - Includes global trade management  

---

### Goals of Supply Chain Optimization
1. Inventory optimization  
2. Improved shipping and order fulfillment time

5. Cost reduction with better customer service

## 6. Revenue and Profitability
- **Revenue = Quantity × Selling Price**
- **Profitability = Profit After Tax (PAT)**

Business structure matters:
- Listed companies vs family-run businesses
- Governance directly impacts scalability

## Key Takeaway
Supply Chain is not logistics.
